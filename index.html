Today I began the Intro to Programming on App Academy Open. In itself, that's not such a notable activity, but when I got to the part on "methods," I was left wondering why aren't those called procedures. It was quite a surprise to read the follow: "Many languages distinquish between functions, which have no associated object, and methods, which are invoked on a receiver object. Because Ruby is a purely object oriented language, all methods are true methods and are associated with at least one object."
